1. If you were to describe semantic HTML to the next cohort of students, what would you say?
	If a website were a body, the semantic HTML is the skeleton. It only includes the major parts necessary for a site,
	like <p> and <header>.
	 

2. Name two big differences between display: block; and display: inline;.
	Display: block will take each element and make its own row. Display: inline fits into the middle of things. 
	Like <p> versus <span>.

3. What are the 4 areas of the box model?
	Content, Padding, Border, Margin

4. While using flexbox, what axis does the following property work on: align-items: center?
	Align-items moves things vertically.

5. Explain why git is valuable to a team of developers.
	Everyone can work on the same project at once and share their changes without altering the original
	version.
